Project Overview Our project is a low-cost, unified platform designed specifically for Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities to solve two massive everyday problems: unpredictable public transport and slow civic complaint resolution. Instead of forcing local governments to buy expensive infrastructure, our platform uses everyday smartphones and QR codes to bring smart-city features to underserved areas at a fraction of the cost. 🛠️ Key Features 1. Smart Mobility & Transit Tracking Low-Cost Live Tracking: Bus drivers use a lightweight mobile companion app to stream their live GPS location, completely eliminating the need for expensive tracking hardware inside the buses. Instant QR-Code Passenger Info: We place static QR codes at bus stops. Commuters can simply scan the QR code with their smartphones to instantly view live bus locations, routes, and ETAs on a clean web portal without downloading any app. 5km Proximity Alert & Notification: Users can search for their specific bus route on the platform and set an alert. As soon as the live GPS shows the bus is within a 5km radius of their stop, the system automatically sends a notification/SOS alert. This gives commuters exactly enough time to head to the bus stop without waiting in the sun or rain. 2. Smart Civic Reporting Geo-Tagged Photo Uploads: Citizens can report local issues like potholes, broken streetlights, water leakages, or garbage piles by simply taking a photo. The platform automatically tags the exact GPS coordinates and time, sending it directly to a centralized municipal dashboard. AI-Powered Backend: To prevent municipal offices from getting overloaded, an AI model automatically analyzes the uploaded photos to: Detect and filter out duplicate complaints for the same issue based on proximity. Assess the severity of the damage. Automatically route the ticket straight to the responsible government department, cutting down manual paperwork. Implementation Strategy (The Roadmap) To ensure this project is highly feasible and easy to build, we have divided the development into two distinct phases: Phase 1: The Core MVP (Immediate Deployment) Focuses entirely on the core tracking system, driver app setup, bus stop QR codes, the 5km proximity alert system, and the basic geo-tagged photo upload feature. This phase requires almost zero upfront infrastructure costs from the government. Phase 2: The Advanced AI Layer (Future Scale-Up) Integrates the computer vision models for automated complaint routing, duplicate detection, and predictive data analytics to help authorities optimize bus schedules based on commuter demand.
